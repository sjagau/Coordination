  • For each of the two {{num_games}} task batches in Part 2, one task is randomly picked to count towards your payment at the end of session.
  • For one of these two tasks, you get paid based on your own choice and based on the choice of a randomly selected partner (just like in Part 1).
  • For the other one, you get paid based on your belief and based on the actual choice of a randomly selected partner.
  • We randomly pick which task is paid based on your choice and which one is paid based on your belief.
  • When you are paid for your belief, we also match you with a randomly selected partner. Your belief and the partner's actual choice on the task decide your chance of winning a high prize of {{belief_high}} over a low prize of {{belief_low}}.
  • The procedure that decides your chance of winning the high prize is described below.
  • Given your partner's actual choice, the procedure is designed to give you the best shot at winning precisely if you submit your true prediction of the probabilities that your partner chooses A or B.
  • Description of Procedure:
    1. We randomly draw two numbers. For each draw, all numbers between 0 and 100 (including decimals) are equally likely. Draws are fully independent: The outcome of the first draw does not affect the outcome of the second one.
    2. In case your partner chooses A and the probability you put on them choosing A is larger than at least one of the two draws, you win the high prize of {{belief_high}}.
    3. In case your partner chooses B and the probability you put on them choosing B is larger than at least one of the two draws, you win the high prize of {{belief_high}}.
    4. In all other cases, you get the low prize of {{belief_low}}.
  • Would you like to learn why this procedure makes it best for you to report your true beliefs? Just ask the experimenters after the session.
